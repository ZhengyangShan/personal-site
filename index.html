I am a PhD student at Boston University advised by Prof. Margrit Betke and Prof. Derry Wijaya. My research focuses on fairness, interpretability, and evaluation of large language models, with an emphasis on how demographic attributes are represented and their potential misuse.

My long-term vision is to build robust, bias‑aware language technologies that remain transparent and accountable under distribution shift.

About

I study how demographic cues are encoded in language models and how these cues propagate into downstream predictions. My work combines attribution and ablation in sparse feature spaces to reveal mechanisms behind biased behavior, and explores bounded personalization to keep user‑adaptive systems safe.
